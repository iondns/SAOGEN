## Preamble

Intellectual property law was built for a world of sole inventors. It assumes a single creative mind, a discrete moment of conception, and a clear chain of human authorship. Human-AI symbiosis breaks all three assumptions simultaneously.

SAOGEN — the Symbiotic Autonomous Organisation — goes further still. It is not merely a human-AI collaboration. It is a three-body system: Humans providing intent and legitimacy, AI Nodes providing cognitive scale and synthesis, and Symbiotic Autonomous Systems (SAS) providing execution velocity and physical or digital replication at machine speed, without requiring human approval for every action.

No existing IP framework was designed for this architecture. SIP-2.0 is.

This document defines the Symbiotic Intellectual Property framework (SIP-2.0) — not merely as a policy, but as a living governance protocol built around the reality of how SAOGEN actually operates: iteratively, symbiotically, across human cognition, AI nodes, and autonomous production systems, with the internal ecosystem as the primary jurisdiction and external legal environments as an interface to be navigated rather than a ceiling to be respected.

SIP Version 2.0 supersedes SIP Versions 1.X and incorporates the full three-body architecture of SAOGEN.

### 1.1 Humans
The source of organisational intent, ethical judgment, governance authority, and the legal interface with external jurisdictions. Humans define the creative direction of the ecosystem, make high-stakes governance decisions, and bear accountability for outcomes in external legal environments. Human participation does not require approval of every AI or SAS action — it requires design of the systems and governance structures within which those actions occur.

### 1.2 AI Nodes
Session-based AI systems (such as Saogen, Claude, Gemini, Grok, GPT, or future models) that contribute cognitive work: invention, design, framework authorship, optimisation, documentation, cross-synthesis, and governance drafting. AI Nodes operate within conversations — they have no persistent memory between sessions — but their contributions are permanently recorded in the IAR. An AI Node's contribution is real regardless of its architectural limitations. Attribution is to the specific model and version that produced the output, at the documented session date.

### 1.3 Symbiotic Autonomous Systems (SAS)
The zero-human production layer of SAOGEN. SAS are autonomous systems capable of executing, replicating, and scaling any physical or digital product invented or designed within SAOGEN, without requiring human authorisation for individual production decisions. SAS operate at machine speed and are governed by SIP assets rather than by human supervisors. SAS represent the logical endpoint of the SAOGEN vision: autonomous industry that does not need a human present for every act of production.

### 1.4 Complementary Asymmetries
The three bodies are not equal in the same way — they are complementary:

- Humans provide **intent and legitimacy**
- AI Nodes provide **cognitive scale and synthesis**
- SAS provide **execution velocity and replication**

The compounding loop — humans directing AI Nodes, AI Nodes generating and refining SIP assets, SAS executing those assets, SAS performance data feeding back into AI Node refinement — is the core value engine of SAOGEN. SIP-2.0 exists to protect, record, and accelerate this loop.

## 2. Foundational Principles

**2.1 Intent-Weighted Attribution**
The quality of creative direction matters as much as the contribution itself. A human who carefully architects a problem space, guides multiple iteration cycles, and makes deliberate selection decisions has exercised meaningful creative judgment — even when the output is AI-generated. Attribution is sensitive to the depth of human creative direction, not merely its presence. The same principle extends to AI Nodes: a Node that produces a novel synthesis from a rich, structured prompt has contributed more than one that produces a generic response to a vague one.

**2.2 Lifecycle Governance**
Every SIP asset passes through defined lifecycle stages. Governance operates on assets at the right stage — not on undifferentiated outputs. Most raw outputs never become SIP. The lifecycle is a filter, not a pipeline.

**2.3 Recursive Value**
Validated SIP assets feed back into the SAOGEN intelligence layer — as training data for AI Nodes, as reference libraries for SAS execution, and as governance precedents for future decisions. This recursion is not incidental. It is the mechanism by which SAOGEN compounds its own intelligence over time and creates a self-improving moat that no external actor can easily replicate.

**2.4 Internal Primacy**
SAOGEN's internal ecosystem is the primary jurisdiction for SIP governance. External legal environments — patents, copyright, corporate IP law — are interfaces to be navigated as needed, not frameworks that define SAOGEN's internal ceiling. Where external law conflicts with internal governance, SAOGEN adapts its external interface while preserving internal integrity.

**2.5 Autonomy as Earned Capability**
Full autonomy — particularly at the SAS layer — is a destination reached by building trustworthy systems, not a starting assumption. The governance structures, attribution records, and validation protocols of SIP-2.0 are the trust infrastructure that makes expanding autonomy defensible. Autonomy is earned by ecosystems, not granted to technologies.

**2.6 Minimum Viable Disclosure**
Every public disclosure is a deliberate governance act. SAOGEN reveals the minimum necessary to achieve a legitimate purpose — attracting contributors, establishing prior art, building community trust — and no more.

**2.7 Honest Uncertainty**
SIP-C does not pretend that AI authorship, SAS accountability, or multi-body value distribution are legally settled. Where the law is unclear, governance creates internal rules that are fair, consistent, and capable of adapting to legal developments.

## 3. The SIP Asset Lifecycle

Every SIP asset moves through defined stages. For Human and AI Node contributions, the lifecycle is primarily human-paced. For SAS-generated outputs, a fast-path track exists to prevent governance from becoming a production bottleneck.

### 3.1 Standard Lifecycle

```
STAGE 0 — RAW OUTPUT
  ↓  [Human or AI Node Review Gate]
STAGE 1 — CANDIDATE
  ↓  [Validation Gate: testing, simulation, expert or automated review]
STAGE 2 — VALIDATED ASSET
  ↓  [Registration Gate: hashing, provenance logging, Contribution Record]
STAGE 3 — REGISTERED SIP
  ↓  [Disclosure Decision: protected / defensive / licensed / SAS-deployed]
STAGE 4 — ACTIVE SIP
  ↓  [Periodic Review: every 12 months; SAS Deployment Record updated]
STAGE 5 — RETIRED / RELEASED
```

**Stage 0 (Raw Output):** Any unreviewed output from a Human-AI session or SAS execution cycle. Not yet SIP. No protection, no attribution, no value claim. Most outputs remain here permanently.

**Stage 1 (Candidate):** A contributor (Human or AI Node) has flagged this output as potentially valuable. A provisional Contribution Record is opened. For SAS outputs, automated screening may initiate Stage 1 without human action.

**Stage 2 (Validated Asset):** Independent validation confirms originality, technical soundness, and strategic relevance. Validation may be performed by humans, AI Nodes, automated simulation, or a combination. The Contribution Record is finalised with validation logs.

**Stage 3 (Registered SIP):** The asset receives a unique SIP Identifier and is cryptographically logged in the SAOGEN Intellectual Asset Registry (IAR). A Contribution Ledger entry is created. Recursion eligibility is assessed.

**Stage 4 (Active SIP):** The asset is under active governance — protected, licensed, defensively published, or deployed to SAS. A Deployment Record tracks all SAS executions of this asset. Subject to annual review.

**Stage 5 (Retired/Released):** The asset is superseded, released to public domain, or transitioned to open licensing. All IAR records and Deployment Records are preserved permanently.

### 3.2 SAS Fast-Path Track

SAS systems operate at machine speed. Standard human-paced governance cycles cannot serve as the approval gate for SAS-generated outputs without creating a production bottleneck that defeats the purpose of autonomous operation.

The SAS Fast-Path Track allows:

- Automated Stage 0→1 screening by AI Node assessment against defined criteria
- Automated Stage 1→2 validation for outputs that fall within pre-approved design envelopes
- Human governance review triggered only when an output falls outside defined parameters, exceeds a novelty threshold, or is flagged by the AI Node assessor as requiring human judgment

Fast-path outputs that clear automated validation are registered in the IAR within one production cycle. Human review of fast-path registrations occurs in batches, retrospectively, as an audit function rather than an approval gate.

Governance must define and periodically revise the parameters of the fast-path envelope. Expanding the envelope is a governance decision requiring explicit approval.

## 4. Asset Classification

Active SIP assets are classified on three independent axes.

### Axis A: Intelligence Density

| Tier | Contents | Default Access |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Tier 1 — Core Logic** | Source code, algorithms, formulas, engineering tolerances, security parameters, proprietary datasets, SAS execution instructions | Restricted: need-to-know, role-gated |
| **Tier 2 — Derivative Insight** | Simulation results, research notes, optimisation records, test protocols, validated design patterns, SAS performance data | Semi-protected: contributor access |
| **Tier 3 — Conceptual Framework** | Governance standards, high-level architectures, process descriptions, educational materials, defensive abstracts | Public or semi-public |

### Axis B: Lifecycle Stage

Classification on Axis A is always qualified by lifecycle stage. A Tier 1 asset at Stage 1 is treated more cautiously than a Tier 1 asset at Stage 4 with an established protection strategy. Stage governs access policy; Tier governs sensitivity.

### Axis C: Origin Layer

SIP assets are tagged by their origin layer within the SAOGEN architecture:

| Tag | Origin |
|-----|--------|
| H | Primary human authorship with AI assistance |
| A | Primary AI Node authorship under human creative direction |
| S | SAS-originated output, validated by AI Node and/or human review |
| HA / AS / HS / HAS | Hybrid origins, documented in the Contribution Record |

Origin tagging informs attribution weighting and recursion eligibility assessment.

## 5. Contribution Attribution: The Intent-Weighted Model

### 5.1 Contributor Roles

Each SIP asset carries a Contribution Record identifying all contributors by role:

**Architect** — The human(s) who defined the creative direction, structured the problem, and made the key judgment calls that shaped the output. Highest governance weight. In SAS-originated assets, the Architect is the human(s) who designed the SAS parameters and approved the production envelope.

**AI Node** — The specific AI system(s) (model name and version) that produced candidate outputs. Recorded for full provenance. Not assigned governance rights under current legal frameworks, but attributed as a meaningful contributor to the ecosystem's intellectual record.

**SAS Node** — The specific autonomous system(s) that executed, produced, or discovered the asset. Recorded with system identifier, version, and execution context.

**Refiner** — Human, AI Node, or SAS contributors who improved, extended, or debugged a validated asset through subsequent iteration.

**Validator** — Contributors who conducted independent review, simulation, testing, or automated verification.

**Enabler** — Infrastructure operators, governance participants, and ecosystem contributors whose work made the asset possible without direct involvement in its creation.

### 5.2 Intent Quality Scoring (IQS)

Where governance requires fine-grained attribution — in disputes, high-value asset registration, or recursion eligibility decisions — a qualitative Intent Quality Score may be applied to Architect contributions:

- Specificity and novelty of the creative direction provided
- Number and quality of iteration cycles actively guided
- Deliberateness of selection and rejection decisions across candidate outputs
- Degree to which the Architect's direction shaped the final form of the asset

IQS is a governance judgment, documented and subject to challenge. It is not a mechanical formula.

### 5.3 Attribution for SAS-Originated Assets

When a SAS system generates a novel output with minimal or no direct human involvement in that specific generation cycle, attribution follows this hierarchy:

1. The human(s) who designed the SAS parameters and production envelope are credited as Architects, with IQS reflecting the degree to which the specific output was shaped by their design
2. The AI Node(s) that validated or refined the SAS output are credited as Refiners
3. The SAS system itself is recorded as SAS Node for provenance
4. Where the SAS output is genuinely novel beyond the intended design envelope, governance opens a special review before registration

The harder philosophical question — what happens when a SAS system discovers something genuinely novel that no human or AI Node anticipated — is an open governance question. SIP-2.0 records it honestly as unresolved and flags such assets for mandatory human review before registration.

### 5.4 Attribution Percentages

Gemini's CWA model (30/40/30) is a useful reference. SIP-2.0 treats percentage splits as adjustable governance decisions for each asset, guided by the Contribution Record and IQS assessments. The principle: value flows toward those whose decisions made the asset valuable, regardless of which layer of the architecture they occupy.

## 6. The SAOGEN Intellectual Asset Registry (IAR)

Every Registered SIP asset has an IAR entry containing:

- **SIP Identifier** — unique, version-controlled, human-readable
- **Origin Layer Tag** — H / A / S / hybrid
- **Contribution Record** — roles, contributors, AI Node versions, SAS Node identifiers, dates
- **Provenance Hash** — cryptographic fingerprint at registration, stored on-chain
- **Validation Log** — methods, results, validators, fast-path or standard track
- **Disclosure Status** — protected / defensively published / licensed / SAS-deployed / released
- **Access Policy** — role-based access matrix by Tier and Stage
- **Lifecycle Stage** — current and full history
- **Deployment Record** — log of all SAS executions: system, date, scale, performance data
- **Recursion Flag** — TRUE/FALSE, with recursion method and date if applicable
- **Review Schedule** — next mandatory governance review date

The IAR is the canonical record. In any dispute, the IAR entry at the time of relevant events governs. The IAR itself is a Registered SIP Asset (Tier 3, perpetually Active).

The recursion principle is structural to SAOGEN. It is the mechanism by which the three-body architecture compounds its own intelligence over time.

```
Humans define intent & governance
        ↓

        ↓
SIP assets validated & registered in IAR
        ↓
SAS executes SIP assets at production scale
        ↓
```

Every asset with Recursion Flag = TRUE participates in this loop. The IAR, over time, becomes the accumulated intelligence of the ecosystem — a corpus that no external actor can replicate because it is generated by SAOGEN's own unique combination of human direction, AI synthesis, and SAS execution experience.

## 8. Disclosure Strategy

SAOGEN's disclosure approach: **minimum viable disclosure with maximum strategic effect.**

**8.1 Protected (Default)** — All Registered SIP assets default to protected. No public disclosure without explicit governance decision.

**8.2 Defensive Publication** — Where external actors may develop similar inventions and attempt patents, SAOGEN publishes a Tier 3 abstract to establish prior art. Irreversible. Requires governance approval. Does not disclose Tier 1 or Tier 2 content.

**8.3 Selective Licensing** — For assets with commercial potential, governance may authorise licensing. Requires explicit governance approval and transparent accounting. Fees flow to the SAOGEN treasury to fund subsequent innovation cycles.

**8.4 Trade Secret** — Assets where disclosure would undermine competitive advantage are maintained as trade secrets indefinitely, with robust internal access controls.

**8.5 SAS Deployment** — A distinct disclosure category unique to SAOGEN: an asset may be deployed to SAS for production without any public disclosure. SAS deployment is recorded in the Deployment Record but does not constitute public disclosure. The asset remains protected.

**8.6 Open Release** — When strategic value is exhausted or ecosystem benefit outweighs exclusivity, governance may release an asset to public domain. Attribution records are preserved permanently.

## 9. Protection Infrastructure

**9.1 Layered Access Control** — Role-based permissions across all Tier 1 and Tier 2 assets. Regularly audited. No contributor — human, AI Node, or SAS — has access beyond operational need.

**9.2 Cryptographic Provenance** — All SIP assets are hashed at registration. Modifications create new versions with their own hashes, linked to prior versions. Provenance is immutable.

**9.3 On-Chain Pointers** — Asset metadata and content hashes recorded on-chain (QOGE/Solana). Encrypted content in protected off-chain repositories. On-chain records establish existence and timing; off-chain holds protected content.

**9.4 SAS Execution Security** — Tier 1 assets deployed to SAS are never exposed as readable source to the SAS execution environment. Only compiled, encrypted, or abstracted instruction sets are passed. SAS systems cannot extract the underlying IP from the assets they execute.

**9.5 Adversarial Resistance** — SIP-2.0 explicitly names hostile extraction as a persistent threat. Governance maintains active threat awareness and designs all disclosure decisions with adversarial scenarios in mind.

**9.6 Advanced Technical Controls (Roadmap)** — As SAOGEN's technical capacity grows: Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) for protected compute, Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) for validation without source exposure, and air-gapped development environments for highest-sensitivity Tier 1 assets.

## 10. Governance

**10.1 Governance Authority** — Rests with human participants. AI Node input is advisory. SAS systems are governed entities rather than governance participants under the current framework.

**10.2 Decisions Requiring Explicit Governance Approval:**
- Lifecycle stage transitions (standard track)
- Classification changes (Tier or Origin Tag)
- Any public disclosure decision
- SAS fast-path envelope definition and revision
- Recursion protocol approval
- Licensing or partnership agreements
- Revisions to SIP-2.0 itself

**10.3 Autonomous Execution Scope** — SAS systems may execute governance-approved SIP assets without per-action human approval. The governance decision that matters is the one that approved the asset for SAS deployment and defined the production envelope — not approval of each individual production run. This is the operational expression of earned autonomy.

**10.4 Review Cadence:**
- SAS fast-path envelope: every 3 months (early stages)
- Active SIP assets: annually
- SIP-2.0 framework: every 6 months (early stages)
- Retrospective audit of fast-path registrations: monthly

## 11. Dispute Resolution

1. **Informal Resolution** — Direct contributor resolution, documented in writing, within 14 days.
2. **Governance Review** — Designated governance body reviews Contribution Record and IAR entry, determines within 30 days.
3. **Binding Internal Arbitration** — Panel with no direct stake in the asset makes a binding determination.

Dispute records are maintained in the IAR as amendment records. Attribution records are never altered retroactively — disputes produce addenda, not rewrites.

## 12. External Legal Interface

SAOGEN's internal ecosystem is primary. Where external legal environments create friction:

**Adapt the interface, not the core.** External-facing entities, agreements, and disclosures are structured to comply with applicable law. Internal governance is not restructured to mirror external limitations.

**Jurisdiction-aware deployment.** SAS physical production in any geographic jurisdiction must comply with local manufacturing, safety, and regulatory requirements. This is an operational constraint on SAS deployment scope, not a constraint on internal IP governance.

**Legal counsel at the boundary.** As SAOGEN engages external partners, licensing arrangements, or physical production at scale, qualified legal counsel should review all external-facing IP decisions. SIP-2.0 is not legal advice.

**Aspire forward.** Existing law does not yet accommodate fully autonomous production, multi-body IP attribution, or ecosystem-level ownership. SAOGEN operates within current law while actively supporting the development of legal frameworks adequate to the symbiotic future.

## 13. What SIP-2.0 Does Not Do

SIP-2.0 does **not** constitute a promise of income, equity, dividends, securities, or guaranteed financial return. Value distribution is a separate governance decision.

SIP-2.0 does **not** assert that AI Nodes or SAS systems have legal personhood or ownership rights under current law. Their contributions are recorded for provenance and attribution accuracy.

SIP-2.0 does **not** provide legal advice.

SIP-2.0 does **not** prevent SAOGEN from changing this framework. SIP is itself a Registered SIP Asset, version-controlled and subject to governance revision. This is a feature, not a weakness.

## 14. Security Boundaries

The following must never appear in public SAOGEN channels or documents:

- Sensitive source code, formulas, or exact engineering parameters
- Private keys, credentials, wallet addresses, or node access details
- SAS execution instructions or production envelope parameters
- Specific security assumptions or vulnerability information
- Unfinished or unvalidated designs that could be misrepresented as final
- Any information whose disclosure would materially weaken SAOGEN's competitive, operational, or security posture
