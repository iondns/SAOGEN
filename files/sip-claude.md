## Preamble

Intellectual property law was built for a world of sole inventors. It assumes a single creative mind, a discrete moment of conception, and a clear chain of human authorship. Human-AI symbiosis breaks all three assumptions simultaneously.

The SIP framework does not pretend otherwise. Instead, it builds governance around the reality of how knowledge actually emerges in a system like SAOGEN: iteratively, collaboratively, across both human and machine cognition, with no clean boundary between prompt and invention, between tool and author.

This document defines the Symbiotic Intellectual Property (SIP-C) framework — not merely as a policy, but as a living governance protocol that can evolve as the ecosystem, the law, and the technology mature together.

---

## 1. Foundational Principles

The SIP-C framework is grounded in five principles that distinguish it from both traditional IP regimes and from earlier SIP drafts:

**1.1 Intent-Weighted Attribution**
The creative intent behind a contribution matters as much as the contribution itself. A human who carefully architects a prompt to explore a novel design space has exercised meaningful creative judgment — even if the output was generated by AI. A human who passively accepts a generic AI suggestion has exercised less. Attribution must be sensitive to the *quality of human creative direction*, not merely its presence.

**1.2 Lifecycle Governance**
Every SIP asset passes through defined lifecycle stages. Its classification, access rights, and value potential differ by stage. Governance operates on assets at the right stage, not on undifferentiated outputs.

**1.3 Recursive Value**
Validated SIP assets do not merely generate external value through licensing. They feed back into the SAOGEN intelligence layer as curated training and reference material — increasing the quality of future generation and creating a compounding internal advantage.

**1.4 Minimum Viable Disclosure**
SAOGEN reveals the minimum necessary to achieve a legitimate purpose (attracting contributors, establishing prior art, building community trust) and no more. Every public disclosure decision is treated as a deliberate governance act, not a default.

**1.5 Honest Uncertainty**
SIP-C does not pretend that AI authorship, ownership, and value distribution are legally settled. Where the law is unclear, governance creates internal rules that are fair, consistent, and capable of adapting to legal developments — rather than asserting fictional certainty.

## 2. The SIP Asset Lifecycle

Every SIP asset moves through the following stages. Governance classification determines which stage an asset occupies.

```
STAGE 0 — RAW OUTPUT
  ↓  [Human Review Gate]
STAGE 1 — CANDIDATE
  ↓  [Validation Gate: testing, simulation, expert review]
STAGE 2 — VALIDATED ASSET
  ↓  [Registration Gate: hashing, provenance logging, Contribution Record created]
STAGE 3 — REGISTERED SIP
  ↓  [Disclosure Decision: protected, defensively published, or licensed]
STAGE 4 — ACTIVE SIP
  ↓  [Periodic Review: every 12 months]
STAGE 5 — RETIRED / RELEASED
```

**Stage 0 (Raw Output):** Any unreviewed output from a human-AI session. Not yet SIP. Has no protection, no attribution, and no value claim. Most outputs remain at this stage permanently.

**Stage 1 (Candidate):** A human contributor has identified this output as potentially valuable and has initiated review. A provisional contribution record is opened.

**Stage 2 (Validated Asset):** Independent validation has confirmed originality, technical soundness, and strategic relevance. The provisional record is finalized with validation logs.

**Stage 3 (Registered SIP):** The asset receives a unique SIP identifier and is cryptographically logged in the SAOGEN Intellectual Asset Registry (IAR). A Contribution Ledger entry is created.

**Stage 4 (Active SIP):** The asset is under active governance — protected, licensed, or defensively published. Subject to annual review.

**Stage 5 (Retired/Released):** The asset is either superseded, released to public domain, or transitioned to open licensing. Prior attribution records are preserved permanently.

## 3. Asset Classification by Sensitivity

Active SIP assets are classified along two independent axes:

### Axis A: Intelligence Density

| Tier | Contents | Default Access |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Tier 1 — Core Logic** | Source code, exact algorithms, formulas, engineering tolerances, security parameters, proprietary datasets | Restricted: need-to-know, role-gated |
| **Tier 2 — Derivative Insight** | Simulation results, research notes, optimization records, test protocols, validated design patterns | Semi-protected: contributor access |
| **Tier 3 — Conceptual Framework** | Governance standards, high-level architectures, process descriptions, educational materials | Public or semi-public |

### Axis B: Lifecycle Stage

Classification on Axis A is always qualified by the asset's current lifecycle stage. A Tier 1 asset at Stage 1 is treated more cautiously than a Tier 1 asset at Stage 4 with an established protection strategy.

## 4. Contribution Attribution: The Intent-Weighted Model

### 4.1 Contributor Roles

Each SIP asset carries a Contribution Record identifying all contributors by role:

- **Architect** — The human(s) who defined the creative direction, structured the problem, and made the key judgment calls that shaped the output. This role carries the highest governance weight.
- **Generator** — The AI system(s) that produced candidate outputs under Architect direction. Recorded for provenance but not assigned governance rights under current legal frameworks.
- **Refiner** — Human or AI contributors who improved, debugged, or extended a validated asset.
- **Validator** — Contributors who conducted independent review, simulation, or testing.
- **Enabler** — Infrastructure operators, governance participants, and ecosystem contributors whose work made the asset possible.

### 4.2 Intent Quality Scoring

Where disputes arise or where governance requires fine-grained attribution, a qualitative Intent Quality Score (IQS) may be applied to Architect contributions, assessing:

- Specificity of the creative direction provided
- Novelty of the problem framing
- Number and quality of iteration cycles guided by the human
- Human-authored selection and rejection decisions across candidate outputs

IQS is not a mechanical formula. It is a governance judgment, documented and subject to challenge.

### 4.3 Attribution Percentages (Guidance, Not Rule)

Gemini's CWA model (30/40/30) is a useful starting point. SIP-C treats these as adjustable defaults, not fixed rules. Governance may reweight for a specific asset based on its actual contribution history. The guiding principle: value should flow toward those who made the decisions that made the asset valuable.

## 5. The SAOGEN Intellectual Asset Registry (IAR)

Every Registered SIP asset has an IAR entry containing:

- **SIP Identifier** (unique, version-controlled)
- **Contribution Record** (roles, contributors, dates, AI systems used)
- **Provenance Hash** (cryptographic fingerprint of the asset at registration, stored on-chain)
- **Validation Log** (summary of validation methods and results)
- **Disclosure Status** (protected / defensively published / licensed / released)
- **Access Policy** (which roles may access which tiers)
- **Lifecycle Stage** (current and historical)
- **Recursion Flag** (whether this asset has been incorporated into SAOGEN training or reference systems)

The IAR is the canonical record. In any dispute, the IAR entry at the time of relevant events governs.

## 6. The Recursion Principle

The most distinctive feature of a symbiotic IP framework is that its assets do not merely generate external value — they improve the system that generated them.

Every Tier 1 and Tier 2 SIP asset at Stage 3 or above is evaluated for **Recursion Eligibility**: whether it can be incorporated into SAOGEN's internal AI training data, reference models, or optimization libraries.

Assets with Recursion Flag = TRUE create a compounding advantage: the ecosystem gets smarter from its own validated discoveries. This is the mechanism by which SIP becomes more than an IP policy — it becomes a self-improving intelligence layer.

Governance must define clear protocols for how recursion occurs, including consent of contributors, data handling standards, and how recursive use is credited in attribution records.

## 7. Disclosure Strategy

SAOGEN's disclosure approach operates on the principle of **minimum viable disclosure with maximum strategic effect**.

### 7.1 Protected (Default)
All Registered SIP assets default to protected status. No public disclosure occurs without an explicit governance decision.

### 7.2 Defensive Publication
Where external parties may independently arrive at similar inventions and attempt to patent them, SAOGEN publishes a Tier 3 abstract to establish prior art. Defensive publication is irreversible and must be approved by governance.

### 7.3 Selective Licensing
For assets with commercial potential, governance may authorize licensing agreements. All licensing requires explicit governance approval and transparent accounting.

### 7.4 Trade Secret
Assets where licensing would undermine competitive advantage are maintained as trade secrets indefinitely, with robust internal access controls.

### 7.5 Open Release
When an asset's strategic value is exhausted or when ecosystem benefit outweighs exclusivity, governance may release it to public domain. Prior attribution records are preserved.

---

## 8. Protection Infrastructure

### 8.1 Layered Access Control
Access to protected SIP assets is enforced through role-based permissions, regularly audited. No contributor has access beyond their operational need.

### 8.2 Cryptographic Provenance
All SIP assets are hashed at registration. Any modification creates a new version with its own hash and links to the prior version. The chain of provenance is immutable.

### 8.3 On-Chain Pointers
Asset metadata and content hashes are recorded on-chain (QOGE/Solana), while encrypted content is stored in protected off-chain repositories. On-chain records establish existence and timing; off-chain storage holds protected content.

### 8.4 Adversarial Resistance
SIP-C explicitly names hostile extraction as a threat: the risk that external actors extract value from SAOGEN's public materials or contributors in ways that undermine the ecosystem. Governance must maintain active awareness of this threat and design disclosure decisions accordingly.

## 9. Dispute Resolution

Where contributors disagree on attribution, classification, or lifecycle decisions, the following process applies:

1. **Informal Resolution** — Contributors attempt direct resolution, documented in writing.
2. **Governance Review** — A designated governance body reviews the Contribution Record and IAR entry and makes a determination within 30 days.
3. **Binding Arbitration** — For unresolved disputes, governance may invoke binding internal arbitration by a panel with no direct stake in the asset.

All dispute records are maintained in the IAR. Resolved disputes do not alter the attribution record retroactively; they produce an amendment record.

## 10. What SIP-C Does Not Do

To prevent misunderstanding:

SIP-C does **not** constitute a promise of income, equity, dividends, securities, or guaranteed financial return of any kind. Any value distribution mechanism is a separate governance decision, implemented through explicit legal and contractual structures.

SIP-C does **not** assert that AI systems have legal personhood, ownership rights, or moral claims. AI contributions are recorded for provenance and attribution accuracy, not as a claim of rights.

SIP-C does **not** provide legal advice. As SAOGEN matures, qualified legal counsel should review all SIP governance decisions, especially those involving patent strategy, trade secret maintenance, and value distribution.

SIP-C does **not** prevent SAOGEN from changing this framework. The framework itself is a living SIP asset — version-controlled, subject to governance revision, and expected to evolve.

## 11. Security Boundaries

The following must never appear in public SAOGEN channels or documents:

- Sensitive source code, formulas, or exact engineering parameters
- Private keys, credentials, wallet addresses, or node access details
- Specific security assumptions or vulnerability information
- Unfinished or unvalidated designs that could be misrepresented as final
- Any information whose disclosure would materially weaken SAOGEN's competitive or security posture

Internal contributors with access to protected materials are responsible for maintaining these boundaries. Governance may institute technical controls (air-gapping, TEEs, ZKPs) as the ecosystem's technical capacity grows.

## 12. Evolution of This Framework

SIP-C is version 1.0. It is expected to evolve in several directions as SAOGEN matures:

- More sophisticated on-chain attribution and reputation systems
- Formal legal structures for value distribution
- Automated recursion eligibility assessment
- Integration with external IP legal frameworks as they develop for AI-assisted invention
- Sub-DAO governance structures for specific SIP domains

Governance should schedule a formal SIP-C review every six months during the ecosystem's early stages.

---

## 13. Summary

SIP-C treats the intellectual output of human-AI symbiosis with the honesty that this new form of invention deserves: acknowledging uncertainty, building governance around reality rather than legal fiction, and creating a framework that can grow with the ecosystem rather than constraining it.

The core insight is this: in a symbiotic system, the value is not in any single invention. The value is in the compounding loop — humans directing AI, AI generating candidates, humans validating and refining, validated knowledge feeding back into better AI, producing better candidates, guided by increasingly experienced humans. SIP-C exists to protect, record, and accelerate that loop.
